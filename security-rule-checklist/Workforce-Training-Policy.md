# Workforce Training Policy

**Policy Title:** Workforce Security Awareness and Training Policy
**Reference:** 45 CFR §164.308(a)(5)
**Applies To:** All staff, contractors, and volunteers with access to ePHI
**Review Frequency:** Annually or after any significant security incident

---

## Purpose

This policy ensures that every person with access to ePHI understands their responsibilities under HIPAA, knows how to recognize security threats, and is equipped to handle patient data safely. Training is not a one-time event. It is an ongoing requirement.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to all workforce members including full-time staff, part-time staff, contractors, temporary staff, and volunteers who access practice systems or patient information in any form.

---

## Training Requirements

### 1. New Hire Training
- All new staff must complete HIPAA security awareness training before they are granted access to any system containing ePHI
- Training must cover: what ePHI is, the minimum necessary rule, password requirements, phishing awareness, device security, and how to report an incident
- Completion must be documented and retained

### 2. Annual Refresher Training
- All staff must complete a refresher training session every 12 months
- The Security Official is responsible for scheduling and tracking completion
- Staff who miss the annual training deadline must complete it within 30 days or have their system access suspended until completion

### 3. Role-Specific Training
- Clinical staff must receive additional training on ePHI handling specific to their patient-facing role
- Administrative staff must receive training on secure handling of billing and scheduling data
- Any staff member given elevated system access (e.g. admin rights) must receive additional technical security training

### 4. Incident-Triggered Training
- Following any security incident, affected staff must complete targeted retraining on the relevant topic within 14 days
- The Security Official determines the scope and content of incident-triggered training

## Training Delivery

Training may be delivered in any of the following formats:
- In-person session led by the Security Official
- Online course through an approved HIPAA training platform
- Written materials with a signed acknowledgement form
- A combination of the above

Regardless of format, completion must be documented with the staff member's name, date, and training content covered.

## Security Reminders

In addition to formal training, the Security Official must issue regular security reminders to all staff. These may be brief and informal but must occur at least monthly. Acceptable formats include:

- A short note in a staff meeting agenda
- A one-paragraph email reminder
- A posted notice in the staff area
- A message in the team communication channel

Reminders should rotate through topics such as phishing awareness, password hygiene, device locking, and proper ePHI disposal.

---

## Violations

Staff who fail to complete required training within the specified timeframe or who demonstrate repeated disregard for security practices are subject to disciplinary action as outlined in the Sanction Policy.
