# Incident Response Policy

**Policy Title:** Incident Response Policy
**Reference:** 45 CFR §164.308(a)(6)
**Applies To:** All staff, contractors, and vendors
**Review Frequency:** Annually or after any security incident

---

## Purpose

This policy defines how the practice identifies, responds to, and documents security incidents involving ePHI. A security incident includes any attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of ePHI.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to all systems, devices, and locations where ePHI is stored, processed, or transmitted.

---

## What Counts as a Security Incident

- Unauthorized access to patient records
- Lost or stolen device containing ePHI
- Phishing email that was clicked by a staff member
- Ransomware or malware infection
- Accidental disclosure of ePHI to the wrong person
- Staff member accessing records outside their job role
- Vendor or third party reporting a breach involving your data

---

## Response Process

### Step 1: Identify and Report
- Any staff member who discovers or suspects a security incident must report it to the Security Official immediately, the same day
- Do not attempt to investigate or fix the issue alone
- Do not delete, alter, or power off affected systems without instruction from the Security Official

### Step 2: Contain
- The Security Official must take immediate steps to stop the incident from spreading
- This may include revoking account access, disconnecting a device from the network, or blocking a suspicious login via Microsoft Entra ID
- Remote wipe of a lost or stolen device must be initiated via Intune within 2 hours of confirmation

### Step 3: Assess
- Determine what ePHI was involved, how many patients are affected, and how the incident occurred
- Document everything: what happened, when, who was involved, and what systems were affected
- Determine whether the incident constitutes a HIPAA breach requiring notification

### Step 4: Notify (if required)
HIPAA breach notification rules apply as follows:

| Situation | Notification Required | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Breach affecting 500 or more individuals | Notify HHS and media | Within 60 days of discovery |
| Breach affecting fewer than 500 individuals | Notify HHS annually | Within 60 days of end of calendar year |
| Affected individuals | Always notify | Without unreasonable delay, within 60 days |
| Business Associate discovers a breach | Notify Covered Entity | Without unreasonable delay |

### Step 5: Remediate
- Fix the root cause of the incident
- Update controls, policies, or training as needed to prevent recurrence
- Document all remediation steps taken

### Step 6: Document and Close
- Complete the Incident Log (see template below) for every incident, regardless of severity
- Retain incident documentation for a minimum of 6 years
