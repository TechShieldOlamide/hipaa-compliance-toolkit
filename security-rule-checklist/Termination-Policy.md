# Termination and Offboarding Policy

**Policy Title:** Workforce Termination and Offboarding Policy
**Reference:** 45 CFR §164.308(a)(3)(ii)(C)
**Applies To:** All staff, contractors, and vendors leaving the practice
**Review Frequency:** Annually or after any termination-related incident

---

## Purpose

This policy ensures that when a staff member, contractor, or vendor relationship ends, all access to ePHI and practice systems is revoked immediately and completely. Failure to offboard properly is one of the most common and preventable causes of unauthorized ePHI access in small practices.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to all separations including voluntary resignations, involuntary terminations, end of contract, retirement, and vendor offboarding.

---

## Vendor Offboarding

When a vendor or Business Associate relationship ends:

- Confirm the vendor has destroyed or returned all ePHI per the Business Associate Agreement
- Revoke any system access granted to the vendor
- Remove the vendor from the BAA register
- Retain the signed BAA for a minimum of 6 years after the relationship ends

---

## Violations

Any staff member responsible for offboarding who fails to complete the required steps on time is subject to disciplinary action. Any former staff member found to have accessed practice systems after their separation date will be reported to the appropriate regulatory authorities.
