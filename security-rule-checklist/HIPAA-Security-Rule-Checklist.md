# HIPAA Security Rule Checklist for Small Healthcare Practices

Reference: 45 CFR Part 164 Subpart C

This checklist covers the administrative, physical, and technical safeguards required under the HIPAA Security Rule, scaled to practices with 1-10 staff. For each item, the "Small Practice Notes" column explains what "reasonable and appropriate" looks like at this size, not at a hospital system.

---

## Section 1: Administrative Safeguards (§164.308)

### 1.1 Security Management Process

| Ref | Requirement | Required or Addressable | Small Practice Notes | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| §164.308(a)(1)(ii)(A) | Risk Analysis | Required | Document all systems that store, process, or transmit ePHI. For a 5-person practice this is typically EHR, email, and staff devices. | ☐ |
| §164.308(a)(1)(ii)(B) | Risk Management | Required | Write down how you will reduce each risk identified. A simple spreadsheet is acceptable. | ☐ |
| §164.308(a)(1)(ii)(C) | Sanction Policy | Required | A short written policy stating that staff who violate HIPAA rules face disciplinary action. One page is enough. | ☐ |
| §164.308(a)(1)(ii)(D) | Information System Activity Review | Required | Review login logs and access reports regularly. Monthly is reasonable for a small practice. | ☐ |

### 1.2 Assigned Security Responsibility

| Ref | Requirement | Required or Addressable | Small Practice Notes | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| §164.308(a)(2) | Security Official | Required | Designate one person as responsible for HIPAA security. In a small practice this is often the practice owner or office manager. Document the designation in writing. | ☐ |

### 1.3 Workforce Security

| Ref | Requirement | Required or Addressable | Small Practice Notes | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| §164.308(a)(3)(ii)(A) | Authorization and Supervision | Addressable | Ensure staff only access ePHI relevant to their role. A receptionist does not need access to clinical notes. | ☐ |
| §164.308(a)(3)(ii)(B) | Workforce Clearance Procedure | Addressable | Background checks before hiring staff with ePHI access. Reasonable for any size practice. | ☐ |
| §164.308(a)(3)(ii)(C) | Termination Procedures | Addressable | Revoke system access on the same day an employee leaves. Document the process. | ☐ |

### 1.4 Information Access Management

| Ref | Requirement | Required or Addressable | Small Practice Notes | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| §164.308(a)(4)(ii)(A) | Isolating Healthcare Clearinghouse Functions | Addressable | Only applies if you also operate as a clearinghouse. Most small practices can mark N/A. | ☐ |
| §164.308(a)(4)(ii)(B) | Access Authorization | Addressable | Document who is authorized to access which systems and why. | ☐ |
| §164.308(a)(4)(ii)(C) | Access Establishment and Modification | Addressable | Have a process for granting, changing, and removing access when roles change. | ☐ |

### 1.5 Security Awareness and Training

| Ref | Requirement | Required or Addressable | Small Practice Notes | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| §164.308(a)(5)(ii)(A) | Security Reminders | Addressable | Regular reminders about security policies. A monthly email or staff meeting note counts. | ☐ |
| §164.308(a)(5)(ii)(B) | Protection from Malicious Software | Addressable | Antivirus on all devices that access ePHI. Defender on M365 satisfies this. | ☐ |
| §164.308(a)(5)(ii)(C) | Log-in Monitoring | Addressable | Train staff to report failed login attempts or suspicious access. | ☐ |
| §164.308(a)(5)(ii)(D) | Password Management | Addressable | Written policy on password length, complexity, and reuse. MFA is strongly recommended. | ☐ |

### 1.6 Security Incident Procedures

| Ref | Requirement | Required or Addressable | Small Practice Notes | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| §164.308(a)(6)(ii) | Response and Reporting | Required | Document how you identify, respond to, and report security incidents. A one-page procedure is acceptable. | ☐ |

### 1.7 Contingency Plan

| Ref | Requirement | Required or Addressable | Small Practice Notes | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| §164.308(a)(7)(ii)(A) | Data Backup Plan | Required | Regular, tested backups of ePHI. Cloud backup via M365 or your EHR vendor counts if configured correctly. | ☐ |
| §164.308(a)(7)(ii)(B) | Disaster Recovery Plan | Required | Written plan for restoring access to ePHI after an incident. Does not need to be complex. | ☐ |
| §164.308(a)(7)(ii)(C) | Emergency Mode Operation Plan | Required | How do you continue critical operations if systems go down? Document it. | ☐ |
| §164.308(a)(7)(ii)(D) | Testing and Revision Procedures | Addressable | Test your backup and recovery process at least once a year. | ☐ |
| §164.308(a)(7)(ii)(E) | Applications and Data Criticality Analysis | Addressable | List your critical systems in order of priority for recovery. For most small practices: EHR first, then email. | ☐ |

### 1.8 Evaluation

| Ref | Requirement | Required or Addressable | Small Practice Notes | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| §164.308(a)(8) | Periodic Technical and Non-Technical Evaluation | Required | Review your security posture when there are major changes (new software, new staff, new devices) or at least annually. | ☐ |

### 1.9 Business Associate Contracts

| Ref | Requirement | Required or Addressable | Small Practice Notes | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| §164.308(b)(1) | Business Associate Contracts | Required | Any vendor that handles ePHI on your behalf (EHR, billing, cloud storage) must sign a Business Associate Agreement (BAA). M365 Business Premium includes a BAA from Microsoft. | ☐ |

## Section 2: Physical Safeguards (§164.310)

| Ref | Requirement | Required or Addressable | Small Practice Notes | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| §164.310(a)(1) | Facility Access Controls | Addressable | Control who can physically access areas where ePHI is stored or accessed. Locked doors and key card or key access is sufficient for most small practices. | ☐ |
| §164.310(a)(2)(i) | Contingency Operations | Addressable | Ensure physical access to systems during an emergency or disaster situation. | ☐ |
| §164.310(a)(2)(ii) | Facility Security Plan | Addressable | A written plan for physically securing your facility and equipment. | ☐ |
| §164.310(a)(2)(iii) | Access Control and Validation Procedures | Addressable | Validate that only authorized staff access sensitive areas. A visitor log is a simple, acceptable control. | ☐ |
| §164.310(a)(2)(iv) | Maintenance Records | Addressable | Keep records of repairs or modifications to physical security controls. | ☐ |
| §164.310(b) | Workstation Use | Required | Written policy on appropriate use of workstations that access ePHI. Screen positioning, locking screens, no personal use on clinical devices. | ☐ |
| §164.310(c) | Workstation Security | Required | Physical measures to protect workstations. Screen locks, cable locks for laptops, devices not left unattended in public areas. | ☐ |
| §164.310(d)(1) | Device and Media Controls | Required | Policies for the receipt, removal, backup, and disposal of devices and media containing ePHI. | ☐ |
| §164.310(d)(2)(i) | Disposal | Addressable | Secure disposal of devices containing ePHI. Remote wipe via Intune before disposal counts. | ☐ |
| §164.310(d)(2)(ii) | Media Re-use | Addressable | Ensure ePHI is fully removed before reusing storage media or devices. | ☐ |
| §164.310(d)(2)(iii) | Accountability | Addressable | Track hardware and media containing ePHI. A simple asset register is sufficient. | ☐ |
| §164.310(d)(2)(iv) | Data Backup and Storage | Addressable | Create retrievable copies of ePHI before moving equipment. | ☐ |

## Section 3: Technical Safeguards (§164.312)

| Ref | Requirement | Required or Addressable | Small Practice Notes | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| §164.312(a)(1) | Access Control | Required | Technical policies to allow only authorized users to access ePHI systems. | ☐ |
| §164.312(a)(2)(i) | Unique User Identification | Required | Every user must have their own login. No shared accounts. | ☐ |
| §164.312(a)(2)(ii) | Emergency Access Procedure | Required | A documented process for accessing ePHI during an emergency when normal access controls are unavailable. | ☐ |
| §164.312(a)(2)(iii) | Automatic Logoff | Addressable | Sessions should time out after a period of inactivity. Configure this in Intune or your EHR. | ☐ |
| §164.312(a)(2)(iv) | Encryption and Decryption | Addressable | Encrypt ePHI at rest and in transit. M365 Business Premium handles this natively if configured correctly. | ☐ |
| §164.312(b) | Audit Controls | Required | Hardware, software, or procedural mechanisms to record and examine activity in systems containing ePHI. M365 audit logs satisfy this. | ☐ |
| §164.312(c)(1) | Integrity Controls | Required | Protect ePHI from improper alteration or destruction. | ☐ |
| §164.312(c)(2) | Mechanism to Authenticate ePHI | Addressable | Verify that ePHI has not been altered or destroyed in an unauthorized manner. File integrity monitoring or version history in M365 counts. | ☐ |
| §164.312(d) | Person or Entity Authentication | Required | Verify that a person or entity seeking access to ePHI is who they claim to be. MFA enforced via Entra ID / Conditional Access in M365 satisfies this. | ☐ |
| §164.312(e)(1) | Transmission Security | Required | Guard against unauthorized access to ePHI transmitted over a network. TLS encryption in M365 covers email. Ensure your EHR uses HTTPS. | ☐ |
| §164.312(e)(2)(i) | Integrity Controls for Transmission | Addressable | Ensure ePHI is not improperly modified during transmission. | ☐ |
| §164.312(e)(2)(ii) | Encryption for Transmission | Addressable | Encrypt ePHI in transit. M365 handles this natively. | ☐ |

## How to Use This Checklist

1. Work through each section with the person designated as your Security Official
2. Mark each item as complete (✅), in progress (🔄), not applicable (N/A), or not started (☐)
3. For every gap you find, document it in your Risk Analysis with a plan to address it
4. Review this checklist at least annually or after any significant system or staffing change
