# HIPAA Compliance Toolkit for Small Healthcare Practices

A practical HIPAA Security Rule compliance toolkit built for solo and small group practices (1-10 staff): dental offices, therapy practices, med spas, and specialty clinics. Most HIPAA guidance online is written for hospital systems and large health networks. This toolkit is scoped for the reality of a small practice: limited IT staff, cloud-first tooling (Microsoft 365 / Google Workspace), and a need for controls that are actually achievable, not just theoretically compliant.

## Why This Exists

A compliant 5-person dental practice looks very different from a compliant 500-bed hospital system. Small practices need to know what is genuinely required under the Security Rule versus what is enterprise best practice that does not fit their context. This toolkit draws that line.

## What Is Inside

| Folder | Contents |
|---|---|
| security-rule-checklist/ | Administrative, physical, and technical safeguards from 45 CFR Part 164 Subpart C, scaled to small practice environments |
| risk-analysis-template/ | A risk analysis template scoped for small practice complexity with a defensible, documented process |
| policy-templates/ | Core policy documents covering access control, incident response, workforce training, and device management |
| m365-intune-mapping/ | Control mapping for practices running Microsoft 365 Business Premium with Intune device management |
| audit-report-template/ | Structured format for writing up findings and producing a client-facing compliance report |

## Who This Is For

- Small practice owners trying to understand what HIPAA actually requires of them
- IT consultants and MSPs supporting healthcare clients
- Compliance professionals scoping their first small-practice engagement

## Important Note

This is a reference toolkit built from HIPAA Security Rule research and compliance framework design experience. It is not a substitute for a completed audit of a live environment and is not legal advice. If you are a practice owner, use this to understand requirements and prepare your evidence. A full audit still requires a qualified professional reviewing your actual environment.
